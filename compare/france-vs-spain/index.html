Our Naming Similarity Index (0–100) combines three components: (1) Shared top 10 — the percentage of names that appear in both countries' top 10 lists (40% weight). (2) Phonetic similarity — how often top names share first-letter and syllable-count patterns (30% weight). (3) Origin cluster overlap — how many names share the same origin country or language in our database (30% weight). Higher scores indicate more similar naming cultures.

Structural Comparison Insight

Structural comparison clarifies how naming cultures diverge. Cultural differences stem from immigration, language, and regional preferences—France and Spain may share language but differ in ethnic mix or naming law (e.g. approved lists). Popularity differences reflect when and where a name peaked; a name popular in one country may be rare or unused in another. Rhythm and syllable patterns matter: some cultures favor shorter names, others longer forms. Style cluster impact—whether names skew classic, modern, or rare—varies by country and influences the overlap score.
